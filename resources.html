Resources

Charities

If you want more information about the places we donate to, here are their websites:

  • Project Linus - a nonprofit which donates blankets to children in need, particularly those in hospitals. There are chapters of this organization in various parts of Massachusetts.
  • Warm Up America! - an organization which gives knitted blankets and hats to hospitals, shelters, and homeless people, as well as victims of disaster.
  • My Very Own Blanket - a charity which donates blankets and pillows to children in foster care.
  • Blanket Heats - a charity which specializes in creating no-sew blankets for homeless people, people in hospitals, and veterans.

Blanket Making Resources

No two blankets are ever made the same way, and we want to help you make a meaningful, unique blanket for someone in need. Here are some tips and tutorials on how to make a wonderful winter blanket:

  • Our No-Sew Blanket tutorial.
  • A Blanket-Making Guide from Blanketed In Love, a chapter of Project Linus.
  • Another guide on making warm blankets from Wool and the Gang, a website that sells various wool, yarn, and knitting supplies
  • Step-by-step instructions on how to make a no-sew blanket by Blanketed In Love
  • A collection of knitting patterns for purchase from Yarnspirations. These patterns are ranked by difficulty and can be bought online.
  • A video on knitting a blanket at the beginner level. Here is the crochet pattern used in the video.
  • Another video on making an Afgan blanket at the beginner level, provided by KristiSimpson.net.
  • a tutorial on making a sewn fleece blanket from a blog called Making Lemonade.
  • a tutorial on making a weighted blanket, which can provide pressure, sensory input, and comfort to those with sensory issues. This tutorial is by Mama Smiles.
  • 23 Crochet patterns of various difficulty provided by Foliver.
